Aim

Quality Improvement Cell is formed to enhance the quality of both the faculty members and the students and It has become essential to improve the teaching skills and knowledge of the teachers and learning process of students to improve quality.

Objectives
  • Quality Improvement cell motivates and aids the faculty to switch from the traditional learning mode to the blended learning mode for the enhancement in quality of technical education.
  • In the blended learning mode, apart from the text books, faculty members have to go through the video lectures provided by eminent scholars / institutions so that teaching – learning process will be effective.
  • Faculty members are encouraged to attend conferences / seminars and workshop to update themselves on present trends. Incentives are provided to encourage their participation.
  • In addition, faculty members are motivated to submit articles in reputed journals and they are awarded for their submission
VISSION

Establishment of an effective internal quality system to enable the NSCET to realize the vision. "NSCET aspires to become an Innovative institution by developing excellent human resources with leadership qualities, ethical and moral values, research culture and innovative skills through higher education of global standards"

MISSION

To design and develop appropriate quality system procedures for academic and administrative activities of the institution in consultation with all the concerned members.

To educate, train, counsel and motivate all the members to effectively implement the developed quality system procedures.

To identify and train a group of internal quality monitoring members to monitor the effective implementation of the quality system procedure.

Functions of QIC
  • Development and application of quality benchmarks/parameters for the various academic and administrative activities.
  • Facilitating the creation of a learner-centric environment conducive for quality education and faculty maturation to adopt the required knowledge and technology for participatory teaching and learning process.
  • Arrangement for feedback responses from students, parents and other stakeholders on quality-related institutional processes.
  • Dissemination of information on the various quality parameters of higher education;
  • Organization of inter and intra institutional workshops, seminars on quality related themes and promotion of quality circles.
  • Documentation of the various programmes /activities of NSCET, leading to quality improvement.
  • Acting as a nodal agency of the NSCET for coordinating quality-related activities, including adoption and dissemination of good practices.
  • Development and maintenance of Institutional database for the purpose of maintaining /enhancing the institutional quality.
  • Development of Quality Culture in NSCET.
  • Preparation of the Annual Quality Assurance Report of the NSCET based on the quality parameters / assessment criteria developed by the relevant quality assurance body (like NAAC, NBA) in the prescribed format.
Goals of QIC
  • To develop a quality system for conscious, consistent and catalytic programmed action to improve the academic and administrative performance of the NSCET.
  • To promote measures for institutional functioning towards quality enhancement through internalization of quality culture and institutionalization of best practices.

ISO Audit

‘ISO 9001 certified’ means an organization has met the requirements in ISO 9001. It defines an ISO 9000 Quality Management System (QMS). ISO 9001 evaluates whether your Quality Management System is appropriate and effective, while forcing you to identify and implement improvements.

ISO 9001 is an internationally Quality Management System. It is suitable for all organizations and will help to improve management processes to compete locally/globally. To achieve ISO 9001 certification we needs to demonstrate that it can meet the regulatory requirements and apply the system effectively to be of recall benefit.

When we are satisfied that our organization is working within the guidelines of the Quality Management System, it is time to call in an ISO 9001 certification Body. The audit can be conducted by a number of different certification bodies.

As part of the internal quality audit preparation, the ISO 9001 auditor will review the ISO 9001 requirements and process documentation defined by the institution for the process to be audited. The ISO 9001 clause for management review inputs requires include:

  • Information on results of audits.
  • Feedback.
  • Process performance.
  • Status of corrective and preventive actions.
  • Follow-up actions from previous management reviews.
  • Changes that could affect the quality management system
  • Recommendations for improvements.
National Board of Accreditation

The National Board of Accreditation, India was initially established by AICTE under section 10(u) of AICTE act, in the year 1994, for periodic evaluations of technical institutions and programs basis according to specified norms and standards as recommend by AICTE council.

NBA in its present form came into existence as an autonomous body with effect from 2010, with the objective of assurance of quality and relevance of education, especially of the program in pharmacy and hospitals, through the mechanism of accreditation of programs offered by technical institutions.

NBA has introduced a new process, parameters and criteria for accreditation. These are in line with the best international practices and oriented to assess the outcomes of the program.
